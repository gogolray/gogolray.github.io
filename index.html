Ray Kawshik

Environmental Humanities • Planetary Cosmopolitics • Literary Theory
“An earthbound in a closed cosmos.”

I work at the intersection of literary studies, environmental humanities, and science studies, examining how literature participates in the making of planetary realities. My research brings together Bruno Latour, Actor-Network Theory, Whitehead, and contemporary planetary fiction to rethink agency, temporality, climate change, and nonhuman beinghood.

About

I am Assistant Professor of English at the Amity Institute of English Studies and Research, Amity University Kolkata. I completed my PhD from the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur with a thesis titled ANT-Ecocriticism in the Anthropocene: Latourian Readings of Select Planetary Fiction 0. My research develops a framework of planetary cosmopolitics that extends Bruno Latour’s Earthbound into a distributed relational ontology attentive to fiction, climate change, and nonhuman articulation.
