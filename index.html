About

My work explores how literature participates in shaping planetary realities. I draw on Bruno Latour, Whitehead, and science studies to rethink agency beyond the human.

I am particularly interested in planetary fiction, multiple temporalities, and the articulation of nonhuman beings within narrative forms.

Research Constellations

Planetary Cosmopolitics

Developing a framework that extends Latour’s Earthbound into a planetary scale of relational existence.

Dalit Feminist Narratives

Examining representational intersectionality and collective subjectivity in autobiographical writing.

Literature & Science Studies

Understanding fiction as an active participant in constructing ecological and cosmopolitical thought.

Selected Work

Planetary (Cosmo)politics in Planetary Fiction

Explores Vandana Singh’s work through climate-altered planetary frameworks.

Comparative Reading of Dalit Women’s Autobiographies

A study of Bama and Baby Kamble through intersectional frameworks.

Teaching

My teaching focuses on interdisciplinary engagement, encouraging students to see literature not as representation, but as participation in world-making processes.
