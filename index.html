About

Scholar · Poet · Visual Ethnographer

I am Assistant Professor of English at the Amity Institute of English Studies and Research, Amity University Kolkata, where I teach Business Communication, Literary Theory, and Modern Indian English Literature.

I completed my PhD from the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur (2015–2022) with a thesis titled ANT-Ecocriticism in the Anthropocene: Latourian Readings of Select Planetary Fiction (CPI 9.67/10). My research develops a framework of planetary cosmopolitics that extends Bruno Latour's Earthbound into a distributed relational ontology attentive to fiction, climate change, and nonhuman articulation.

My work draws on thinkers including Isabelle Stengers, Donna Haraway, Vandana Singh, Rob Nixon, and Amitav Ghosh, integrating Whitehead's process philosophy with postcolonial ecocriticism. I treat fiction not as illustration of theory but as its very ground.

Beyond the academy, I write poetry in Bengali and English, and practice photography as a form of visual ethnography. A documentary photograph from my series Street-Dwellers in Calcutta was published by BBC UK in 2013.

Research

Areas of Inquiry

My research spans environmental humanities, planetary fiction, posthumanism, and Dalit feminist studies — united by a commitment to relational ontology.

Primary

Planetary Cosmopolitics

Extending Latour's Earthbound into a distributed relational ontology. Fiction as cosmopolitical laboratory for thinking nonhuman agency and collective futures.

Primary

Environmental Humanities

Climate change, Anthropocene studies, slow violence, ecological ethics. Reading planetary crisis through literature, science studies, and postcolonial theory.

Primary

Actor-Network Theory

Latourian readings of literary and cultural texts. ANT-Ecocriticism as a methodological framework for tracking nonhuman political actors.

Secondary

Dalit Feminist Studies

Intersectionality, representational politics, and collective subjectivity. Reading Bama and Baby Kamble through Crenshaw's intersectional framework.

Secondary

Sanskrit Aesthetics & Relational Ontology

Sahitya as relational ontology — reading Abhinavagupta alongside Whitehead, Barad, and Latour for a nonmodern aesthetics beyond representation.

Secondary

Process Philosophy

Whitehead's philosophy of organism in dialogue with posthumanism and Anthropocene theory. Temporality, event, and multiple worldhoods.
